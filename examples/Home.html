inspiration
From the beginning we were fascinated by the endless possibilities synthetic biology grants. Anything that you can imagine can be done, with the right amount of:
research, knowledge, time, dedication and creativity.
This made us look for ways to expand synthetic biology’s outreach while maintaining high ethical, technical and safety standards. The foundational advance track was a clear path ahead to fulfill our goal.
Once settled in this track we started researching and chose a problem that we believe is so ingrained within the standard development process of genetic circuits that it has been overlooked. Even further, the possible applications that a different way of thinking, designing, constructing and developing genetic circuits might enable are difficult to come by to say the least. It was evident that
a tool, a framework, a foundation was needed.
Hopefully the tools we develop in this project, and the project itself become the foundation of many other projects to come. It’s this hope that inspires to do the best work we can think of, and maybe more.
Objective
Objective
To pursue an incipient focus on genetic circuit development, based on sequential logic, that can enable a whole new set of genetic functions and expand the possible applications for engineered organisms, making way for the scientists of tomorrow to continue in this line of research.
problem
Currently genetic circuits being developed are biased to their core by combinatorial logic meaning that most functions are achieved by specific input - output relationships which limits functionality, circuit architecture and possible applications since logic gates are rigid, complex when combined and unresponsive to a key element: TIME
Solution
Solution
We propose a framework based on sequential logic. Which is much more similar to nature’s, responsive to time and state, and whose potential has not yet been fully exploited, serving as tool for new implementations and applications.
